Journal Entry: The Hidden Saint

“Charity is not enough; we need social reform.” Pier Giorgio lived this belief every day. His family’s wealth did not shield him from the harsh realities others faced. Rather than indulgence, he chose service—visiting the sick, sharing meals with the poor, and championing Catholic values.

Blog: His Love for Laura

Pier Giorgio’s affection for Laura Hidalgo was deeply heartfelt but ultimately unfulfilled. Despite his love, he chose silence, fearing family disapproval and discord. This sacrifice revealed his maturity and moral strength at a young age.

Reflections on Beatitudes

Saint John Paul II called him “The Man of the Beatitudes.” Frassati embodied every aspect of Christ’s call—from peacemaker to merciful soul, showing us that holiness is lived, not declared.

Family and Background

Parents

Adelaide Ametis Frassati – A renowned painter who exhibited at the Venice Biennale. Known for her artistic talent and support of Pier Giorgio’s growth. Died at 72 in 1949.

Alfredo Frassati – Founder of La Stampa, lawyer, senator, and ambassador to Germany. A strong political figure, yet distant father. Lived to 92, passing away in 1961.

Siblings

  • Elda Frassati – Died in infancy at eight months old.
  • Luciana Frassati Gawrońska – His younger sister, lived to 105. A WWII anti-Nazi activist and author of his biography.

Luciana’s Family

Married to Jan Gawroński, Polish diplomat. They had seven children, including Wanda and Piotr Jerzy. Luciana continued Frassati’s legacy through her writings and service.

Extended Family

  • Aunt Elena Ametis – Helped raise the children in the Frassati household.
  • Aunt Emilia Ametis – Died of a heart attack at 20 years old.
  • Aunt Emma Frassati – Tragically murdered by her fiancé; memorialized in Pollone.
  • Aunt Giulietta & Uncle Pietro Frassati – Siblings of Alfredo.

Grandparents

  • Maternal: Francesco & Linda Ametis
  • Paternal: Pietro & Giuseppina (Josephine) Frassati

Friendships

Pier Giorgio was surrounded by a vibrant community of friends, including Marco Beltramo and his sister Luciana. He once wrote:

“Every day I ought to thank God because He has given me men and lady friends of such goodness who form for me a precious guide for my whole life.”
