Welcome to Frassati's Legacy

Blessed Pier Giorgio Frassati (1901–1925) lived a short yet impactful life filled with faith, joy, and dedication to the poor. Born into an influential family in Turin, Italy, he chose the path of humility and service, embodying the Gospel in daily life. This website honors his life, virtue, and voice—a beacon for youth, laypeople, and social reformers alike.

Life at a Glance

  • Born: April 6, 1901 in Turin, Kingdom of Italy
  • Died: July 4, 1925 (Age 24, from polio)
  • Occupation: Engineering student, social activist, member of the Third Order of Saint Dominic
  • Nicknamed: "Man of the Beatitudes" by Pope John Paul II
  • Canonization: On August 3, 2025

Why He Inspires Millions

Frassati did not perform miracles in the traditional sense, but his life was a miracle of compassion. He spent his energy climbing mountains and descending into the streets of poverty, bringing food, medicine, and Christ to those in need. His blend of spiritual depth and practical action makes him a saint for our modern times.

What is the Jubilee Year 2025?

The concept of "Jubilee" has its roots in the Book of Leviticus (chapter 25) as a unique year of reconciliation, pilgrimage, and coming home. 2025 is the Jubilee Holy Year, the 2,025th anniversary of the Incarnation of our Lord, a "event of great spiritual, ecclesial, and social significance in the life of the Church." The Holy Year of 2025 has been proclaimed by Pope Francis as a moment to reaffirm our commitment to being "Pilgrims of Hope."

About the Creators

This website was created by a group of senior high school students from the University of Santo Tomas. It is dedicated to sharing the inspiring life and enduring kindness of Blessed Pier Giorgio Frassati, while also celebrating the upcoming Jubilee Year of 2025. More than just a source of information, this website is a beacon of hope. It also offers a glimpse into the personal faith journeys of each student behind this project—stories of growth, purpose, and devotion. We are here to share positivity, love, kindness, and hope with everyone. Verso L'Alto!
